Naya Lab Sequencing Database

Summary about our database

This database is for scientists interested in the sequencing data obtained in the Naya lab at Boston University. The objective of Dr. Naya’s research is to perform large scale sequencing on mutant muscle cells to investigate the epigenetic mechanisms of cell state transitions in differentiation. They have performed CUT & RUN experiments for two different histone markers, KMD5A and Meg3, and ATAC-seq experiments to investigate general chromatin accessibility. From these experiments, they have provided us with two types of sequencing data: CUT & RUN Sequencing and ATAC-sequencing. The user will be able to type in a gene and the database would return quantitative information on the difference in chromatin states between different cell lines based on Dr. Naya’s data. Additionally, the database is able to display the coverage of each type of data across the murine genome in the form of a line chart.
