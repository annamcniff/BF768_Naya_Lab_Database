Naya Lab Sequencing Database

Summary about our database

This database is for scientists interested in genomic data from CRISPR-modified skeletal muscle cell lines generated by the Naya lab at Boston University. The objective of Dr. Naya's research is to investigate noncoding RNA (ncRNA)-mediated epigenetic regulation of cell state transitions in muscle differentiation. The Naya Lab has mutated the imprinted Dlk1-Dio3 ncRNA locus in C2C12 myoblasts and performed CUT & RUN experiments for two different histone markers, H3K27me3 and H3K4ac, and ATAC-seq experiments to investigate general chromatin accessibility. From these experiments, they have provided us with two types of genome-wide sequencing data: CUT & RUN Sequencing and ATAC-sequencing. The user will be able to type in a gene and the database would return quantitative information on the difference in chromatin states at genomic loci between different cell lines based on Dr. Naya's data. Additionally, the database is able to display the coverage of each type of data across the murine genome in the form of a line chart.
